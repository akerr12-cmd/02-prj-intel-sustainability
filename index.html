Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution.

1978

8086 Processor

The 8086 launches, establishing the x86 architecture that powers modern computing.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 32‑bit 386 processor, enabling advanced performance and multitasking.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

Intel reaches its highest operational emissions, then accelerates investment in abatement and renewable energy.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE strategy and 2030 goals, driving progress in climate action and waste reduction.

2022

Net-Zero by 2040

Intel commits to achieving net‑zero Scope 1 and 2 emissions across global operations by 2040.

2023

Renewable Electricity

Intel reaches 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, dramatically reducing emissions.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting leaders to advance sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
